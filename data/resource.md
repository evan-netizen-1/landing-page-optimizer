# Homegrown — Product & Business Context for Landing Page Optimization

## What Is Homegrown

Homegrown is the simplest way for local food vendors (farmers market sellers, cottage food producers, homesteaders, small farms) to sell online. Vendors get a branded storefront, order management, payments, and automatic sales tax handling — all for $10/mo.

## Pricing

- $10/mo billed annually, $12.50/mo billed monthly
- 7-day free trial, credit card required upfront
- Everything included — no tiers, no add-ons, no transaction fees beyond Stripe's standard rate

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) — 3 Segments

85% of signups are ICP 1-2 (not market vendors). Copy must speak to ALL three.

**ICP 1 — The Social Seller (easiest to convert, highest volume)**
Selling homemade goods via Instagram DMs, Facebook Marketplace, Nextdoor. Taking payment through Venmo/Cash App. Tracking orders in their head or a notebook. Doesn't think of themselves as "a business" yet. Competing tools: Venmo + DMs, Bakesy, Bake Shop, Castiron, Google Forms. They need VALIDATION — "you're ready for this." Biggest fear: looking presumptuous by having a "store." Biggest desire: being taken seriously.

**ICP 2 — The Farmstand / Porch Pickup Seller (sweet spot, best retention)**
Has a farmstand, porch pickup, or regular local route. Takes orders via text messages. Has 10-50 regular customers. Demand exceeds their ability to manually coordinate. Competing tools: basically nothing (texts + cash). They need EFFICIENCY — "this replaces your texts." Biggest fear: customers won't use it. Biggest desire: stop forgetting orders and wasting product.

**ICP 3 — The Market Vendor (hardest to convert, stickiest once converted)**
Has a booth at 1-3 farmers markets. Uses Square POS. Might have a Shopify/Wix website that gets 2 orders/month. Competing tools: Square Online, Shopify, Local Line, Barn2Door. They need COMPARISON — "this vs what you have." Biggest fear: another tool that doesn't work. Biggest desire: revenue between market days.

**Common traits across all 3:**
- One-person or family business
- Revenue $5K-$100K/year from their food business
- Already selling (DMs, texts, markets, or farmstand) — Homegrown removes friction, doesn't create their business
- Not tech-savvy, mobile-first — manage everything from their phone
- Care deeply about personal customer relationships

## Key Value Props (ranked by customer resonance)

1. **Simplicity** — "Live in 15 minutes" is the #1 hook. These vendors have tried Shopify and given up.
2. **Price** — $10/mo vs $29-$124/mo for alternatives. For vendors making $500-2000/mo, cost matters.
3. **Sales tax handled** — This is the #1 pain point that competitors don't solve. Vendors dread tax season.
4. **Marketplace discovery** — 700+ vendors on the Homegrown marketplace means free customer acquisition.
5. **Built for vendors, not enterprise** — Simple tools for one-person businesses, not complex dashboards.

## Customer Objections (by ICP)

**ICP 1 (Social Seller):**
1. "I don't sell enough to need a tool" → At $10/mo, one extra order pays for it
2. "My customers are fine with DMs" → Until you forget an order or someone doesn't pay
3. "I'm not a real business yet" → You're already selling. This just makes it easier.
4. "What if nobody orders from my store?" → Text the link to 5 regulars. They will.

**ICP 2 (Farmstand/Porch):**
5. "My customers like texting me" → They'll still text you. But now they can also order themselves.
6. "Online ordering seems too corporate" → It's just a link — simpler than what you're doing now
7. "I can just take orders via text/DM" → That works until you have 20+ orders/week and forget three people

**ICP 3 (Market Vendor):**
8. "I already have a website" → Does it take pre-orders and handle pickup scheduling?
9. "I already use Square" → Square at the booth is great. This fills the gap between market days.
10. "I tried online ordering and it didn't work" → What you tried wasn't built for pickup-style selling.
11. "What about delivery?" → Homegrown is pickup-focused. Customers order online, pick up at your booth or location.

## Messaging Constraints (MUST follow)

- **$10/mo is accurate** — never change this number
- **700+ vendors is accurate** — never inflate or change
- **No false urgency** — don't add countdown timers, "limited spots," etc.
- **No income claims** — don't promise specific revenue amounts
- **Keep it honest** — Homegrown is simple and affordable, not a magic solution
- **Mobile-first copy** — headlines under 8 words, subheadlines under 20 words. Most vendors read on phones.

## CRO-Informed Mutation Strategy

### Tier 1 — Biggest impact (test first)

**Value proposition reframes (must work for all 3 ICPs):**
- Current frame: convenience ("Order Anytime") — OK for all
- Test: universal simplicity ("One Link. Your Customers Order & Pay.") — works for DMs, texts, AND markets
- Test: identity validation ("You're Already Selling. Now Get the Right Tool.") — speaks to ICP 1-2
- Test: pain amplification ("Stop Losing Orders in Your DMs and Texts") — ICP 1-2 direct
- Test: outcome ("Know Exactly What to Prepare. Get Paid Before Pickup.") — ICP 2-3
- Test: price anchoring ("The $10 Storefront That Runs Itself") — universal
- AVOID: "Between markets" framing in headlines — alienates 85% of signups who aren't at markets

**CTA text (value over action):**
- Current: "Start Selling Today"
- Test: "Get Your Free Storefront" (value-focused)
- Test: "See Your Store in 5 Minutes" (speed + curiosity)
- Test: "Join 700+ Vendors Selling Online" (social proof)
- Test: "Create Your Store Link" (specific outcome — resonates with ICP 1)

### Tier 2 — High impact

- **Social proof specificity** — "738 vendors in 28 states" beats "700+ vendors"
- **Subheadline as objection-handler** — Address "I already have a website" directly
- **Feature framing** — Lead with outcomes ("Get paid before market day") not features ("Online payments")

### Tier 3 — Medium impact

- Section headline reframes
- Comparison section angle changes

### Mutation Prompt Principles

1. **Reframe, don't rephrase** — Every challenger must test a fundamentally different psychological frame
2. **One big hypothesis per experiment** — State: "This tests whether [frame] converts better than [current frame]"
3. **CRO frameworks:** Loss aversion, social proof, scarcity, specificity, objection-handling, benefit-over-feature, curiosity gap
4. **Personalized CTAs perform 202% better** — Make CTAs speak to the visitor's situation
5. **Mobile-first copy** — Headlines under 8 words, subheadlines under 20 words
