# Homegrown — Product & Business Context for Landing Page Optimization

## What Is Homegrown

Homegrown is the simplest way for local food vendors (farmers market sellers, cottage food producers, homesteaders, small farms) to sell online. Vendors get a branded storefront, order management, payments, and automatic sales tax handling — all for $10/mo.

## Pricing

- $10/mo billed annually, $12.50/mo billed monthly
- 7-day free trial, credit card required upfront
- Everything included — no tiers, no add-ons, no transaction fees beyond Stripe's standard rate

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Primary:** Farmers market vendors who sell weekly at 1-3 markets and want to take orders between market days. They're not tech-savvy, often running their business from a phone. They already have loyal customers who ask "can I order between markets?"

**Secondary:** Cottage food producers, small farms with farmstands, homesteaders selling eggs/honey/produce.

**Common traits:**
- One-person or family business
- Revenue $5K-$100K/year from their food business
- Already selling at farmers markets or local events
- Customers ask them for online ordering
- Frustrated by Shopify/Square complexity and cost
- Mobile-first — manage everything from their phone

## Key Value Props (ranked by customer resonance)

1. **Simplicity** — "Live in 15 minutes" is the #1 hook. These vendors have tried Shopify and given up.
2. **Price** — $10/mo vs $29-$124/mo for alternatives. For vendors making $500-2000/mo, cost matters.
3. **Sales tax handled** — This is the #1 pain point that competitors don't solve. Vendors dread tax season.
4. **Marketplace discovery** — 700+ vendors on the Homegrown marketplace means free customer acquisition.
5. **Built for vendors, not enterprise** — Simple tools for one-person businesses, not complex dashboards.

| Competitor | Price | Pain Point |

## Customer Objections (from sales conversations)

1. "I already have a website" → Homegrown isn't a website builder. It's an ordering system. Works alongside your existing site.
2. "I don't sell enough to need this" → At $10/mo, you only need 1-2 extra orders/month to cover the cost.
3. "My customers aren't tech-savvy" → If they can text, they can order. It's a simple link.
4. "I can just take orders via text/DM" → That works until you have 20+ orders/week. Then you need a system.
5. "What about delivery?" → Homegrown is pickup-focused. Customers order online, pick up at your market booth or location.

## Messaging Constraints (MUST follow)

- **$10/mo is accurate** — never change this number
- **700+ vendors is accurate** — never inflate or change
- **No false urgency** — don't add countdown timers, "limited spots," etc.
- **No income claims** — don't promise specific revenue amounts
- **Keep it honest** — Homegrown is simple and affordable, not a magic solution
- **Mobile-first copy** — headlines under 8 words, subheadlines under 20 words. Most vendors read on phones.

## CRO-Informed Mutation Strategy

### Tier 1 — Biggest impact (test first)

**Value proposition reframes:**
- Current frame: convenience ("Order Anytime")
- Test: loss aversion ("Stop Losing Sales Between Market Days")
- Test: customer demand ("Your Regulars Want to Order Online. Let Them.")
- Test: price anchoring ("The $10 Storefront That Runs Itself")
- Test: identity ("You're a Business Owner, Not Just a Market Vendor")

**CTA text (value over action):**
- Current: "Start Selling Today"
- Test: "Get Your Free Storefront" (value-focused)
- Test: "See Your Store in 5 Minutes" (speed + curiosity)
- Test: "Join 700+ Vendors Selling Online" (social proof)

### Tier 2 — High impact

- **Social proof specificity** — "738 vendors in 28 states" beats "700+ vendors"
- **Subheadline as objection-handler** — Address "I already have a website" directly
- **Feature framing** — Lead with outcomes ("Get paid before market day") not features ("Online payments")

### Tier 3 — Medium impact

- Section headline reframes
- Comparison section angle changes

### Mutation Prompt Principles

1. **Reframe, don't rephrase** — Every challenger must test a fundamentally different psychological frame
2. **One big hypothesis per experiment** — State: "This tests whether [frame] converts better than [current frame]"
3. **CRO frameworks:** Loss aversion, social proof, scarcity, specificity, objection-handling, benefit-over-feature, curiosity gap
4. **Personalized CTAs perform 202% better** — Make CTAs speak to the visitor's situation
5. **Mobile-first copy** — Headlines under 8 words, subheadlines under 20 words
